Backdoors

Backdoors are a remote access into a computers programming used by both hackers and administrators. (WIRED)Backdoors are usaly built into the hardware of a computer for use by the government or the creature of the computer. The backdoors are most offen built in without the consumers notice and are frequently used by hackers to get into peoples private computers and once they are in they have full access of the compuer. Administraters uselly build these backdoors for their own use so they can come and go and do what they want (WIRED). The administrators can also allow other people into the computer such as the government so they can also see your private data.


Privacy Over Security

Privacy over security is the arguement that a citizens personal privacy is greater than security being able to access their private data and see what they are doing. The government actually supports their citizens having most of their data be private if it is not harmful fo rthe common good (Privacy). Public opinion and some company opinions prefer privacy over security such as in the case where apple refused to open a terrorists Iphone for information when asked to by the government (Apple). This shows that majority of opinions want to keep their privacy and don't want their data to be looked at by anyone.
